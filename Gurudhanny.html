Gurudhanny - Goanna




Gurudhanny is the Totem of the Eastern Wiradjuri people of Central NSW, Australia.

  • Genus: Varanus
  • Diet: Carnivorous Reptile
  • Habitat: Open woodlands and grasslands, near water in swampy areas and in the deserts.
  • Tradition: For those who share this Totem, to eat or harm Gurudhanny could bring upon sickness or death, while to other Indigenous language groups goannas are an important traditional native food source and are commonly represented in Indigenous Dreaming stories throughout Australia.

Varied in colour, shape and size the Goanna radiated from the north to Africa and Australia during the Miocene epoch, around 15 million years ago and have an important place in our history and culture. 25 of the 80 known species of Varanus can be found here in Australia.
